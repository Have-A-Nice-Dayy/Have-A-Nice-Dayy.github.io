Step 1: Clock Module

Input(s) 1 2 2 4

Vcc min/max 4.75V/7V 4.75V/7V 4.75V/7V 2V/15V
Combinations 4 4 4 N/A
Figure 1 - Clock Module

In order to make a CPU, you need a clock and this module allows you to choose between a monostable clock or a astable clock with the a bistable circuit controlling the switch between the two types of clocks. The definition of a bistable circuit is that it is either on or it is off so, the state is stable. The definition of a astable circuit is that the state on or off is unstable; constantly switching between both on and off states. A monostable circuit means that when pressed it turns on but when let go it turns off, so the state is only on and stable depending on how long you trigger its state.

Below I will post a video of my clock module in action.

In the video the red light indicates you have entered monostable mode which I refer to as manual mode because it allows me to manually advance the clock one cycle. The green light indicates astable mode which means the clocks advances on its own at the set frequency.
